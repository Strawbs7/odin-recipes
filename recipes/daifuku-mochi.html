Daifuku Mochi

Daifuku mochi is a beloved Japanese confection known for its delightful combination of chewy mochi outer layer and sweet filling. Imagine a delicacy that marries the tender resilience of mochi with a burst of luscious filling, creating a treat that delights the senses and warms the soul. Daifuku mochi, a beloved Japanese confection, embodies this harmonious blend of textures and flavors with its simplicity yet profound indulgence.

Ingredients

Mochi

  • shiratamako (glutinous rice flour/sweet rice flour) or mochiko â€“ I prefer to use shiratamako as it yields a better texture. However, I understand that mochiko is more affordable and widely accessible.
  • 130 mL water
  • 25 g granulated sugar about 2 tbsp
  • potato starch or cornstarch
  • 150-180 g red bean paste
  • 6 strawberries cored

Steps

  1. Divide red bean paste into six equal portions and roll into balls. Each should be 25-30g each or about the size of a ping pong ball. Put in refrigerator while you assemble the mochi dough.
  2. In a microwave safe bowl, mix shiratamako, sugar, and water. Cover and microwave for 2 minutes, or until mochi dough is cooked through.
  3. While still hot, turn out mochi onto a surface dusted with starch. Divide into six equal parts. Roll each part into a flat circle, roughly 3-4" in diameter.
  4. Take one circle, keeping the rest of the mochi covered with a damp towel to keep it from drying out. Place a red bean paste ball in the center, and wrap the mochi around it, pinching the edges to seal into a ball. Roll daifuku in more starch to keep it from sticking.
  5. Repeat with the other five mochi circles and red bean paste balls.
  6. With kitchen scissors, snip the top of each daifuku. Press a cored strawberry into the opening of each daifuku, and you're done! Daifuku need to be eaten the day they are made.
